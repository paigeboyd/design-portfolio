2D: Mapping Prototypes

Below are the two maps I created to choose between for my takahe website. I chose the second map in the end.


I created this map on MapBuildr and used the 'light dream' theme from Snazzy Maps. I chose to get rid of the scale control to save space seeing as the map would be small on the website and to prevent it looking cluttered. This map was zoomed to level 6 to give an overall view of the bottom of New Zealand. For this prototype I decided to keep the map looking quite similar to the original Google Maps map so that the users would instantly recognise it and be aware that it can be interacted with.



This map was created on Snazzy Maps and uses four flat colours to maintain a consistent style with the rest of the website. The larger labels were removed and the city/park names were reduced in size because the map will be quite small on the webpage and these made it look cluttered. The map is zoomed in to level 7 and the marker is positioned at (-45.4416668, 168.6920145) which is at Murchison Mountains, South Island, New Zealand. I chose to keep the zooming controls on it to allow the user to interact with the map more, as opposed to only being able to drag the map which is what I had considered prior to creating this.

In the end I chose to use this map because I liked the colours better for fitting within my website style yet still standing out enough as to not blend into the background image. I also liked how the labels were reduced and made smaller and felt that this was a better choice in terms of it's zoom level and overall feel.
